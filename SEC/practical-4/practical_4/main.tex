\section{Introduction}

Figures and graphical illustrations play an important role in technical
documents. They help readers understand complex concepts quickly by
presenting information visually. Diagrams, charts, and images allow
authors to communicate data and processes more effectively than plain
text alone \cite{tufte2001}.

Visual representations are particularly useful in scientific and
engineering reports. A well-designed figure can summarize large amounts
of information in a concise and intuitive way. This improves both the
clarity and readability of the document \cite{lamport1994}.

\section{Example Figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.5\textwidth]{fire&blood.jpg}
\caption{Symbol of house of the dragon}
\label{fig:example}
\end{figure}

As shown in Figure~\ref{fig:example}, graphical elements provide a
visual reference that helps readers grasp the context of the discussion.
Figures are typically centered, numbered automatically by LaTeX, and
accompanied by captions that describe their content.

\section{Text Wrapping Example}

\begin{wrapfigure}{r}{0.4\textwidth}
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.35\textwidth]{fire&blood.jpg}
\caption{Wrapped image example}
\label{fig:wrap}
\end{wrapfigure}

Text wrapping around images is another useful formatting technique.
It allows the document to use space efficiently while still presenting
visual content. When figures are placed alongside text, readers can
refer to them without interrupting the flow of reading.

\section{Conclusion}

In technical writing, figures enhance comprehension, improve document
structure, and make information easier to interpret. When combined with
clear captions and proper references, they significantly improve the
quality of technical communication.
